Frequently asked

The government reference price is the official indicative rate published by the Ministry of Agriculture (MAFC), Tanzania Coffee Board, Cotton Development Trust and Cashewnut Board. It reflects the regulated fair market value. The supplier price is what the individual seller is actually offering. FURROW shows both side by side so any buyer can instantly see who is above or below the official rate — and by exactly how much.
Prices are updated daily by the FURROW administration team from official ministry publications. The timestamp at the top of the price board shows when data was last confirmed. The website automatically checks for new data every five minutes — no page reload needed.
Yes. FURROW is open to international participants. During registration, select “Non-Tanzanian” and choose your country from the full world dropdown. You can browse all listings and use FURROW’s Mediation service to verify stock and handle cross-border documentation — phytosanitary certificates, TRA clearance, export permits, and bill of lading — without travelling to Tanzania.
5% of the deal value for Tanzania local transactions. 10% for cross-border or international deals.
